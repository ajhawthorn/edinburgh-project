Edinburgh's top attractions

  • Edinburgh Castle - a fortress with spectacular views across the city and the Firth of Forth. A must-see attraction in Edinburgh to soak up Scottish history and culture. Climb the castle hill and enter across the drawbridge to explore the dungeons, the Great Hall where banquets were held, the Stone of Destiny, and the Crown Jewels. Not forgetting the One O'clock Gun fired every day across the city.

  • Royal Botanic Gardens Edinburgh - One of the more peaceful parts of Edinburgh with free entry to over 70 acres of beautiful gardens. The Royal Botanic Gardens Edinburgh is a world-renowned centre of excellence in plant science and conservation horticulture with highlights including the Woodland Garden, Chinese Hillside, Rock Garden, and Herbaceous Border. Stop off for lunch or coffee at the Gateway Cafe, Terrace Cafe, or the East Gate Coffee Bar.

    0 Likes

  • Arthur's Seat - In the centre of Holyrood Park, next to the Palace of Holyroodhouse and the Scottish Parliament, lies Arthur's Seat, an extinct volcano in the heart of the city. Walk to the top to enjoy views across Edinburgh and beyond. Would recommend good walking shoes as some of the steep, stony paths can be slippery, but the view from the top is worth the hike!
